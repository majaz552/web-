Bachelor's Degree (B.E./B.Tech):


Bachelor of Engineering (B.E.) or Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech) degrees are undergraduate programs typically completed in four years. These programs provide a
broad foundation in engineering principles and usually offer specializations in fields such as
mechanical engineering, electrical engineering, civil engineering, computer science and engineering, chemical engineering, and more.
Bachelor's degrees in engineering often include a combination of coursework, laboratory sessions, and practical

Master's Degree (M.E./M.Tech):


Master of Engineering (M.E.) or Master of Technology (M.Tech) degrees are graduate programs completed after obtaining a bachelor's degree.
These programs usually focus on advanced topics within specific engineering disciplines and may require students to conduct research or complete a thesis.
Master's degrees in engineering provide students with opportunities for specialization and in-depth study of particular areas of interest.
